# Nextdoor — Ready-to-Post Copy

Three angles for Jack to post on Nextdoor. Mix and rotate. Don't post the same variant in three neighborhoods; Nextdoor throttles repetitive content.

**Rules of engagement:**
- Post in Jack's own neighborhood first, then expand outward week by week.
- Avoid posting as a Business unless verified. Posting as a neighbor reads more authentic.
- No ALL-CAPS subjects, no emoji in subjects, nothing that looks like a Craigslist ad.
- Reply to every comment within 4 hours during the first 48 hours after posting. Engagement = reach.
- After 3 days, the post is stale. Repost a different variant in the same or a neighboring 'hood.

**Body:**
Hi neighbors —

I'm not selling anything yet. I just want feedback from real owners running real shops. If you're up for a 20-minute coffee or Zoom and a free 30-day trial, DM me. I'll come to you.

— Jack, [your neighborhood]

## Variant 2 — The problem-first angle

**Subject:** HVAC owners: how many applicants did you call back this week?

**Body:**
Genuinely curious — for folks here running a home service business (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, etc.), what does your hiring process actually look like these days?

I keep hearing from owners that they post on Indeed, get 10 applicants, and only manage to call 2 back before the rest take other jobs. The "ghosting" problem seems to be hitting everyone.

I'm building a small tool to help with this — an AI that calls every applicant within 60 seconds and screens them so you only meet the ones worth your time. Looking for 3–5 local shops to try it free for a month and tell me what works and what doesn't.

Hit me up if your last hire took longer than it should have. I'd love to hear what you tried.

— Jack

## What to track from Nextdoor

Per post, log in a simple spreadsheet:
- Neighborhood
- Variant used
- Date posted
- Views (visible on Nextdoor)
- Comments
- DMs received
- DMs → call booked
- Call → 30-day pilot started

After 3 posts, the best-performing neighborhood × variant combo will be obvious. Double down there.

## Beyond Nextdoor — same playbook, more channels

The Nextdoor copy is the seed. Adapt it for:

- **Local Facebook contractor groups** — "[City] HVAC Contractors", "[State] Trade Business Owners". Near-identical copy.
- **r/HVAC, r/electricians, r/plumbing** — flip the angle. Ask owners what AI tools they'd find useful, then pitch.
- **ABC / PHCC / ACCA local chapter newsletters** — pitch a free workshop on "AI hiring for small shops" + give attendees free pilot access.
- **Texas Trade Association events** — show up, hand out cards with the QR code to flexforce.ai.
- **Trade school career fairs** — talk to placement officers, not students. They know every shop in town that's hiring.

The 500K email list will outperform all of the above combined once segmented and activated (see README section 4). Nextdoor is the warm-up — the proof Jack can articulate the pitch in plain language to a real local owner before scaling outbound.
